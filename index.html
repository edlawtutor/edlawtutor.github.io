About Me

Hello, my name is Ed and I am a math tutor. I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Northwestern University and I previously worked in the automotive industry as a senior engineering specialist for 28 years. In my spare time, I worked with elementary through high school students coaching FIRST Robotics in my kids’ school district for 8 years. After taking an early retirement and moving to the bay area, I decided to continue doing what I love: working with students.

I tutored my two kids since they were in elementary school. I worked with them on math all the way through high school. As a result, they were able to fully understand these concepts and excelled in the classroom. They both earned a 4.0 GPA and top percentile standardized test scores. After receiving offers from many top schools, both of them chose to attend Stanford University.

In addition to math tutoring, I also work with students and parents on all the challenges associated with applying to colleges including ACT/SAT Test Prep. Recently having gone through the same process with both of my kids, I know how much stress there is regarding college admissions. I found that just a little guidance can go a long way for a student and their parents.

Because I am retired and not planning to be a full time tutor, my availability is extremely flexible. No matter what it is, from last minute quiz reviews to long test-preparation sessions, feel free to shoot me a message and I will find the availability to accommodate you. If interested, I recommend a free “get-to-know-you” session with the parents and the students to assess the student’s exact needs, set goals and expectations, and create a plan to ensure success. Looking forward to hearing from you!

Elementary Math

The most effective way to teach math to young kids is through a hybrid of the mastery approach used in many Asian countries and the mindset approach used in many western countries. This hybrid approach can produce the most results in the shortest time. I used it with both of my own kids who studied math 3 years ahead of their class. I also like to make math fun by using games to do the repetition rather than using approach like Kumon. I am using this approach with my elementary math students with great success. Mastering the basic arithmetic and concepts is very important for later math subjects.

Pre Algebra

I would put 6th, 7th and 8th graders in this group. I use a very different approach with these students. I currently have 5 students in this category. With my system, I use this time to stretch their mind, teach them to see patterns, solve problems by thinking outside the box and make math fun. As we encounter topics they have not learned, we will cover the topics thoroughly. Of course, it is very important that students have a firm command of fractions arithmetic before starting pre-algebra.

Algebra 1

Students who studied with me in pre-algebra for at least a year will find Algebra 1 easy. That is because I may have already covered quite a bit of it. For a new student in algebra, I need to first make sure they are solid with fractions arithmetic and prime factorization. Algebra is not a hard math subject because there are rules to follow and it is easy to learn these rules.

Geometry

This is a very important subject because roughly 40% of ACT math and 30% of SAT math involves geometry. Geometry proofs which are required study under the CA Common Core curriculum is not tested in ACT or SAT. However students need to know the theorems and postulates well in order to excel in ACT and SAT math.

Algebra 2

Algebra is the most important math subject because roughly 40% of ACT math and 35% SAT math is algebra. It is important to make sure skills from Algebra 1 are mastered before going to Algebra 2. Most of the time, students in Algebra 2 need help because of something they forgot in Algebra 1.

Trigonometry

This is not taught as a standalone math subject in the US. Trigonometry may be taught as part of geometry, algebra 2, pre-calculus or combination of them depending on the school. The trigonometry that is tested in ACT and SAT math is very simple. I taught students techniques to quickly derive all the trigonometric functions of special angles without memorizing the unit circle.

Pre-Calculus

Many students who had been doing fine with math in school suddenly found that they were having trouble with their pre-calculus class. This is very common because pre-calculus is the first time math becomes more abstract (especially with limits) and there are no clear steps to follow to solve math problems like in previous math classes. Many of my students told me that they like how I explained the concepts which are easier to understand comparing to how they learned it in school.

Calculus

Many of my students told me that they like how I explained the Calculus concepts which are easier to understand comparing to how they learned it in school. I was able to breakdown complicated calculus concepts into easy to understand lessons. It helps to de-mystify a scary subject and allow the students to be more confident going into taking a test.

SAT Math

Whether it is in a small class setting or private tutoring, the standard approach of tutoring SAT Math seems to be reviewing math materials and work on as many problems as possible from an SAT practice book. From my experience, this type of preparation is time consuming and with limited success. My proven method is very different. After a last minute two hour lesson, one student took SAT a second time and the SAT Math improved from 590 to 650. After just 10 lessons, another two of my students improved from mid 600s to mid 700s. I am currently working with a few other SAT Math students.

ACT Math

Whether it is in a small class setting or private tutoring, the standard approach of tutoring ACT Math seems to be reviewing math materials and work on as many problems as possible from an ACT practice book. From my experience, this type of preparation is time consuming and with limited success. My proven method is very different. After 7 one-hour lessons, one student improved the ACT Math from 24 to 30 and another one improved from 25 to 32. I am currently working with a few other ACT Math students.
